The New England Seamounts Acoustics (NESMA) experiment is an Office of Naval
Research program studying how ocean variability near the New England
Seamounts — where the Gulf Stream interacts with rugged deep-ocean
topography — shapes underwater sound propagation.

C4PO's role is observational: our glider team conducted autonomous glider
operations during the NESMA field campaigns, working alongside partners
including the Woods Hole Oceanographic Institution to characterize the
mesoscale and submesoscale structure of the region's temperature and
salinity fields.
