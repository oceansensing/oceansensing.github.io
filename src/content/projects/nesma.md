The New England Seamounts Acoustics (NESMA) experiment is an Office of Naval
Research program studying how ocean variability shapes underwater sound
propagation near the New England Seamounts, where the Gulf Stream meets
rugged deep-ocean topography. C4PO's role is observational: working from
the sailing research vessel *Marie Tharp* with the Ocean Research Project,
our glider team conducted autonomous SeaExplorer glider operations during
the 2024 NESMA field campaigns — alongside partners including the Woods
Hole Oceanographic Institution — to characterize the mesoscale and
submesoscale structure of the temperature and salinity fields through which
the experiment's acoustic signals travel.
